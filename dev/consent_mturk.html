Title of the Study: The perception of objects and events -- online experiments

Principal Investigator: Emily Ward (phone: 608-262-9945) (email: ejward@wisc.edu)


DESCRIPTION OF THE RESEARCH

You are invited to participate in a research study about perception and attention.

You are invited to participate in a research study about perception and attention. You have been asked to participate because our research relies on individuals like you to help us understand how human perception works. Your participation in this study is extremely valuable for our research.

The purpose of the research is to better understand how visual information from the environment is perceived and used to guide behavior.

This study will include individuals recruited from the Amazon Mechanical Turk worker pool.

The experiment will be presented in your web browser.
 

WHAT WILL MY PARTICIPATION INVOLVE?

If you decide to participate in this research you will be asked to examine a computer display with some simple visual stimuli, and will respond by pressing some keys or using the computer mouse.

Your participation will last approximately 1 - 15 minutes total. The exact length was outlined in the Mechanical Turk HIT information.


ARE THERE ANY RISKS TO ME?

We don't anticipate any risks to you from participation in this study.


ARE THERE ANY BENEFITS TO ME?

We don't expect any direct benefits to you from participation in this study.


WILL I BE COMPENSATED FOR MY PARTICIPATION?

You will receive approximately ~$7.25/hr for completing this study.

 

HOW WILL MY CONFIDENTIALITY BE PROTECTED?

This study is anonymous. Neither your name or any other identifiable information will be recorded.
